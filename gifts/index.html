Care

A few small habits, to help the flowers last.

When the bouquet arrives, cut 1–2 cm from each stem at an angle and place it in a clean vessel of fresh water. Refresh the water every two days.

Keep them out of direct sun and away from heat and fruit — bananas and apples especially. Both quietly speed wilting.

With care, most blooms keep their form for 5–7 days. Hydrangeas and garden roses — the patient ones — can hold for ten days or more in the right spot.

How much lead time do you need?

Three days is typical — enough time to source and design well. Same-day delivery is possible when confirmed by 12 PM PT, with a rush fee for afternoon delivery. Next-day rush fees are disclosed upfront.

How does delivery work?

Calculated by distance, hand-delivered across Orange County from our Newport Coast atelier. Greater distances are quoted per project.

Can I send a reference photo?

Yes. With three decades of hotel-trained practice behind us, we match palette, scale, and proportion closely. If a seasonal stem isn't available the day of, we'll let you know and substitute with one of equal or greater value and similar character.

Can I see the arrangement before it ships?

Before each delivery, we text you a photo for approval — what you see is what arrives.

What's your refund policy?

Floral work is custom and perishable, so we don't accept returns once an arrangement has been delivered.
