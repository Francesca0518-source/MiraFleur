Care

Helping the flowers last.

When you receive the bouquet, cut 1–2 cm from each stem at an angle and place it in a clean vessel of fresh water. Refresh the water every two days.

Keep them away from direct sun, heat, and fruit — especially bananas and apples, which speed wilting.

With care, most flowers will live 5–7 days. Hydrangeas and garden roses, the patient ones, can last 10 days or more in the right spot.

How much lead time do you need?

Three days is typical, so we can source and design properly. Same-day delivery is possible if confirmed by 12 PM PT — afternoon delivery, rush fee may apply. Next-day rush fees are disclosed upfront.

Calculated by distance. We hand-deliver across Orange County from our Newport Coast base. Greater distances quoted per project.

Yes. With three decades of hotel-trained craft behind us, we match palette, scale, and proportion closely. Some seasonal stems may be unavailable on the day; if so, we'll let you know and substitute with stems of equal or greater value and similar character.

What's your refund policy?

As floral work is custom and perishable, we do not accept returns once arrangements have been delivered.
